Sustainability

All about using resources wisely in such a way that we are able to meet our demands without compromising the ability of future generations to fulfill their own needs

Ecological Footprint

Tracks how much biologically productive area it takes to provide for all the competing demands of people, Including space for food growing, fiber production, timber regeneration etc

Biocapacity

Measured by calculating the amount of biologically productive land and sea area available to provide the resources a population consumes & to absorb its wastes, given current technology and management practices.

Ecological Debtor and Creditor

A country has an ecological reserve if its Footprint is smaller than its biocapacity; otherwise it is operating with an ecological deficit. The former are often referred to as ecological creditors and the latter ecological debtors.

Background of the Study

The Ecological Footprint is the only metric that measures how much nature we have and how much nature we use. The Footprint helps: countries (improve sustainability and well-being), local leaders (optimize public project investments) and individuals (understand their impact on the planet).

Data Source

Authenticity

The calculations in the National Footprint and Biocapacity Accounts are based on UN affiliated datasets, including those published by renowned agencies

Unit of Measurement

The Footprint is measured in global-acres (GHA), units of biologically productive area necessary to produce the annual flow of resources that are consumed
